# Chart Patterns Reference — Indian F&O

How to use: identify the pattern, then require its **confirmation trigger**
before calling it tradable. A pattern without confirmation is "forming,"
not "formed" — say which. Targets are measured moves; SL is always the
pattern's invalidation level.

## 1. Candlestick Patterns (single & multi-candle)

### Reversal — Bullish
| Pattern | Structure | Confirmation | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hammer | Small body, lower wick ≥ 2× body, at support/downtrend low | Next candle closes above hammer high | Stronger with volume spike |
| Inverted Hammer | Upper wick ≥ 2× body after downtrend | Bullish close next candle | Weaker than hammer alone |
| Bullish Engulfing | Green body fully engulfs prior red body | Close of engulfing candle itself | Best at a demand zone / put-OI support |
| Piercing Line | Green closes above midpoint of prior red | Next candle holds above midpoint | |
| Morning Star | Red → small-body gap/pause → strong green | Third candle closes above first candle's midpoint | High reliability at swing lows |
| Tweezer Bottom | Two candles with equal lows | Bullish close on second | Marks precise support |
| Three White Soldiers | Three consecutive strong greens with higher closes | Pattern itself | Avoid chasing the third candle — wait for pullback |
| Dragonfly Doji | Open=close at high, long lower wick | Next bullish candle | |

### Reversal — Bearish
| Pattern | Structure | Confirmation | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Shooting Star | Upper wick ≥ 2× body at uptrend high | Next candle closes below its low | Best at call-OI resistance |
| Hanging Man | Hammer shape at a top | Bearish close next candle | |
| Bearish Engulfing | Red body fully engulfs prior green | Close of the engulfing candle | |
| Dark Cloud Cover | Red closes below midpoint of prior green | Next candle stays below midpoint | |
| Evening Star | Green → small body → strong red | Third closes below first's midpoint | |
| Tweezer Top | Equal highs on two candles | Bearish close on second | |
| Three Black Crows | Three strong reds, lower closes | Pattern itself | |
| Gravestone Doji | Open=close at low, long upper wick | Next bearish candle | |

### Continuation / Indecision
- **Doji / Spinning top:** indecision — meaningful only at a level; alone it is noise.
- **Rising/Falling Three Methods:** small counter-trend candles inside a big candle's range, then trend resumes.
- **Marubozu:** full-body candle — momentum conviction; a bullish marubozu through resistance is a breakout candle.
- **Inside Bar:** candle within prior candle's range — trade the break of the mother candle's high/low, SL other side.

## 2. Classical Chart Patterns

### Reversal
| Pattern | Confirmation trigger | Target | Invalidation (SL) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Head & Shoulders | Close below neckline (retest entry is safer) | Head-to-neckline distance projected down | Above right shoulder |
| Inverse H&S | Close above neckline | Same distance projected up | Below right shoulder |
| Double Top (M) | Break of the middle trough | Height of pattern projected down | Above the tops |
| Double Bottom (W) | Break of the middle peak | Height projected up | Below the bottoms |
| Triple Top/Bottom | Break of the confirmation line | Pattern height | Beyond the extremes |
| Rounding Bottom / Cup & Handle | Break of rim/handle high with volume | Depth of cup | Below handle low |
| Rising/Falling Wedge | Break against the wedge's slope | Back of the wedge | Beyond last swing inside wedge |

### Continuation
| Pattern | Confirmation | Target | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bull/Bear Flag | Break of flag channel in trend direction | Flagpole length | Volume dries in flag, expands on break |
| Pennant | Break of converging trendlines | Pole length | Short duration (5–20 candles) |
| Ascending Triangle | Break of flat top | Triangle height | Flat top = repeated call writing being absorbed |
| Descending Triangle | Break of flat bottom | Triangle height | |
| Symmetrical Triangle | Break either side + retest | Height at widest point | Directionless until break — do not pre-position |
| Rectangle / Range | Break of range with volume | Range height | Inside the range, fade edges only with reversal candles |

### Gaps (index futures / stocks)
- **Gap-up/down open:** classify vs previous day's range — *gap & go* (opens beyond PDH/PDL and holds first 15-min range) vs *gap fill* (rejects and fills toward previous close).
- **Breakaway / runaway / exhaustion gaps** by trend position; exhaustion gaps near call/put OI walls often mark expiry-day reversals.

## 3. Price-Action & Market-Structure Concepts

- **Market structure:** uptrend = higher highs + higher lows (HH/HL); downtrend = LH/LL. A **Break of Structure (BOS)** continues the trend; a **Change of Character (CHoCH)** — first break of the opposing swing — is the earliest reversal warning.
- **Support/Resistance:** horizontal levels from swing points, previous day high/low (PDH/PDL), previous week high/low, gap edges, and round numbers (NIFTY 50-point, BANKNIFTY 100-point strikes).
- **Supply/Demand zones:** the base candles before a strong impulsive departure; first retest is highest probability.
- **Trendlines & channels:** need ≥ 3 touches to be tradable; trade the break-retest, not the third touch blindly.
- **Order blocks (SMC):** last opposite candle before an impulsive move that broke structure; refined entry zone within supply/demand.
- **Fair Value Gaps (FVG)/imbalance:** 3-candle gap between candle-1 high and candle-3 low; price often rebalances into it before continuing.
- **Liquidity sweeps / stop hunts:** wick through an obvious equal-high/equal-low or PDH/PDL followed by sharp rejection — classic index move before the real direction; a sweep + CHoCH is an A-grade reversal setup.
- **Fibonacci retracement:** 38.2/50/61.8% of the last impulse for pullback entries in trend; confluence with an OI level or order block upgrades the setup.
- **Volume confirmation:** breakouts need expanding volume (for options, rising futures volume + OI); a breakout on falling volume is suspect — expect a fakeout (see trap patterns below).
- **Traps:** *bull trap* = breakout above resistance that closes back inside (often a short-covering spike into a call wall); *bear trap* = mirror image. The failure candle is itself a tradable signal in the opposite direction.
